CV Goal: Convince the recruiter that you are the person they want to hire

Recruiters scan a CV in 7 seconds to know if they should keep reading or not

## CV parts:
Contact information
	Email
	Phone number
	Location
	Willing to relocate
Education
	Graduation year and school
Work Experience
	Highlight accomplishment
	Action verbs lead, program, direct, evaluate, advise
Skills
	Soft
	Hard/Technical
		Code languages that are used in the job
Links to portfolio
	Easily found

Use language that the job description is using

## CV Presentation
- Portfolios should be easy to find
- Most recent projects first
- CVs from the same school can look the same, how can you stand out?
- CV name: LastName_FirstName
- References are not needed
- Dont put "present" in education, just the future graduate year
- Use Canva for CV templates

## Cover letters
- CV is more fact oriented, cover letters are more literary
- Know the audience of the letter
- Show your personality while being professional
- Header, contact information, introduction about yourself and why you are applying, support information about your candidacy and what you expect.
- Highlight relevant abilities useful for the job. Get the key parts of the job description and explain how you are good for those parts
- Finish by rounding up the letter and indicate your desire for personal interview 
- Cover letter less than one page
- Add some personal connection with the games of the company

## Portfolio
- Just show finished polished pieces
- You are remembered for the worse piece of your portfolio
- Best one first
- If you are putting group projects, specify the areas where you were responsible
